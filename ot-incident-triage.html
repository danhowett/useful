OT Incident Triage · Safety-first decision support

Slow down. Then act.

An IT-trained responder facing an OT alert. The instinct is to isolate, image, investigate — and in OT that instinct can trip safety systems, halt production, or worse. This tool walks you through asset-type triage, safety gates, and produces a decision artefact with explicit allowed and prohibited actions, the calls to make first, and a handover pack for the OT engineering authority.

8 asset types 4 criticality levels 5 safety gates NIST SP 800-82r3 · IEC 62443 · NCSC OT JSON · MD · Print exports
!
Decision support — not engineering authority. Not advice. This tool helps an IT responder think through an OT incident before acting; it does not replace engineering judgement, operations authority, or safety system assessment. You must not rely on this tool's output as a substitute for involving qualified OT engineering, operations, and safety personnel. Decisions about OT assets — especially those connected to safety instrumented systems — have consequences for people and equipment that only your engineering and operations teams can weigh. What are you looking at?

OT triage starts with the asset, not the threat. The asset type determines what's safe to do next. Then layer criticality on top — same asset means different things on a refinery safety loop vs a brewery historian.

Stage 02 / Safety gates

Before you touch anything

Five binary gates. Answer honestly — these aren't ceremony, they determine whether the triage decision is proceed, hold, or full-stop. If you can't answer Yes to all, that's information, not failure.

Safety gates
Answer each as truthfully as your current information allows. "Unknown" is valid and changes the path.
Stage 03 / Triage decision

What the picture says

Based on asset type, criticality, observations, and safety gate answers. Read the rationale — the decision is only as good as the reasoning underneath it.

Stage 04 / Actions & evidence

What you can do, what you must not

Asset-specific allowed actions, the explicit do-not list, evidence collection that's safe to attempt without OT engineering present, and the call list. Read all four before acting.

Stage 05 / Handover & export

Hand over cleanly

The triage pack is what you give the OT engineering lead when they pick up. Capture timestamps, who you've called, what you've done, what you've not done. Then export.

Export the triage pack
Four formats. JSON for ingestion by ICCC and other tools in the suite. Markdown for the handover note. Printable for the OT lead. Architecture snapshot is portable across the suite.
